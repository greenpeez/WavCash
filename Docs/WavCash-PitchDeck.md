## SLIDE 2: THE PROBLEM

Music rights holders in the fastest-growing markets are operating completely
blind.

Royalties are fragmented across five or more platforms, each with separate
dashboards and payment schedules. Rights holders have no way to verify whether
payments are accurate or complete. CMO registration is broken, inaccessible,
or in some markets actively corrupt. Payments take two to six months to arrive.
Converting to local currency costs three to five percent through traditional
banking infrastructure. Split agreements live in WhatsApp messages and verbal
promises.

Rights holders do not know what they are owed. They know what they were paid,
only after the fact, and only sometimes.

## SLIDE 3: DOCUMENTED FAILURES

These are not outliers. They are the norm.

KENYA, 2024

Artist Bien-Aime Baraza received $11.61 from MCSK for millions of streams.
He refused to collect it. There was no explanation of how it was calculated
and no way to verify whether it was correct.

NIGERIA, JANUARY 2026

The Nigerian Copyright Commission attempted to distribute over one billion
naira in private copy levies. Major labels objected publicly. The IFPI wrote
to the Commission warning of irreversible prejudice to rights holders.
The money remains undistributed while artists wait.

SOUTH AFRICA

SAMRO unlocked R22 million in unclaimed royalties simply by updating bank
account records. That money had been sitting in the system, uncollected,
because rights holders did not know it existed.

## SLIDE 4: THE SOLUTION

WavCash: royalty intelligence with instant local payouts.

Aggregate: All royalty sources unified in one dashboard. Every DSP, every
distributor, every CMO, one view.

Verify: A real-time oracle calculates what rights holders should be earning
before any payment arrives.

Detect: Automatic discrepancy detection flags when payouts do not match
verified estimates. Missing money surfaces immediately.

Register: Guided CMO registration. WavCash prepares the complete submission
package. The rights holder submits directly.

Settle: Instant payouts via M-Pesa, PIX, Paystack, bank transfer, or USDC
the moment royalties arrive, not months later.

Split: Permanent onchain agreements that establish who owns what before a
song is released, with automatic redistribution when money arrives.

## SLIDE 6: WHO WE SERVE

Every music rights holder, not only artists. A rights holder is any individual
or entity with a legal ownership stake in a musical work or sound recording.

Independent Artists: Primary acquisition channel. They do not know what they
are owed, cannot verify payouts, and have never registered with a CMO.

Producers and Beatmakers: Get flat fees with no backend. Made the beat for a
song with five million streams and got paid once. Need onchain split agreements
before the song is released.

Songwriters and Composers: Unregistered with PROs. Missing mechanical and
performance royalties that belong to them.

Session Musicians and Engineers: Credits missing from metadata. Not registered
with any CMO. Have played on hundreds of recordings and received zero ongoing
royalties.

Small Labels and Managers (3 or more artists): Reconciling multiple distributor
statements in Google Sheets. Cannot compete on transparency. Losing artists to
labels with better tools.

## SLIDE 8: THE CORE INSIGHT

The payment rails exist. The collection infrastructure does not.

M-Pesa, PIX, and Paystack can move money in seconds. These are not emerging
technologies. They are mature, widely adopted infrastructure already processing
billions of dollars in daily transaction volume.

There is no infrastructure that tells rights holders what they are owed,
verifies they are being paid correctly, or aggregates earnings across the
multiple systems where their money lives.

Traditional royalty infrastructure was built for major labels with legal
departments, US and EU markets with functioning CMOs, quarterly reporting
cycles, and bank wire payments. The fastest-growing music markets need
something different: self-serve tools, real-time visibility, transparent
split agreements, and instant settlement in the currency people actually use.
WavCash is that layer.
